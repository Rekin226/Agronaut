# Common System Failures and Fast Triage

Most aquaponics emergencies are one of a handful of failures. Triage in this order: **oxygen → ammonia
→ flow → temperature**.

## Power outage / pump failure (most dangerous)
No pump = no circulation, no aeration, and the biofilter stops getting oxygen. Fish can die within hours,
and ammonia climbs once nitrification stalls.
- Immediate: restore aeration first (battery air pump), then circulation.
- Prevention: **battery-backup air pump**, ideally an alarm or auto-failover. This is the single highest-value
  insurance in the whole system.

## Overfeeding (most common root cause)
Uneaten feed rots → ammonia spike, more solids, lower DO, worse water.
- Rule: feed only what fish finish in ~5 minutes; remove leftovers; cut feeding during any stress event.

## Solids accumulation / clogging
Sludge builds in pipes, grow beds, and biofilter, creating anaerobic pockets (foul smell, black sludge,
possible H2S) and starving the biofilter of flow.
- Add/clean a solids filter (swirl/settler), flush beds, keep mechanical filtration ahead of the biofilter.

## Biofilter problems
A new, undersized, or damaged biofilter shows up as ammonia and/or nitrite spikes (see nitrogen-cycle doc).
Chlorinated top-up water, medications, or a hot/anoxic spell can kill the bacteria.
- Always dechlorinate top-up water; avoid antibiotics in the loop; protect biofilter oxygen and flow.

## Temperature swings
Mean temperature matters, but so do swings. A heatwave lowers DO and raises ammonia toxicity at once;
a cold snap suppresses appetite and nitrification. Shade, insulate, or heat as the species needs.

## Plumbing: siphons, leaks, blockages
- Bell-siphon grow beds can stick (won't drain = flooding, or won't seal = constant trickle). Check the
  siphon and standpipe first when a bed floods or empties wrongly.
- A dropping water level usually means a leak or evaporation; rising solids or overflow means a blockage.

## Source-water surprises
New or changed source water can bring chlorine/chloramine, high pH/hardness, or salinity. Test and
dechlorinate before topping up, especially after a big water change.

## Fast triage checklist (when "something's wrong")
1. Are fish gasping at the surface? → oxygen first (aerate now).
2. Test ammonia + nitrite → if high, stop feeding + water change.
3. Is the pump moving water? Any clog/leak/siphon stuck? → restore flow.
4. Check temperature and pH/KH for a recent swing.
5. Change one thing at a time; avoid large sudden corrections unless fish are dying.
